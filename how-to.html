How to buy books from a Paperight outlet

Paperight allows any business with any printer to act as a print-on-demand bookstore. If you want to buy a book print-out from a Paperight outlet, here's how it works:

  1. Find a Paperight outlet here. Any organisation with a computer, Internet connection, and a printer can register as an outlet here. It takes a couple of minutes. Ideally, they should have a large laser printer or photocopier-printer, and a way to bind lots of pages, such as a ring-binding machine or binder files.
  2. The outlet then finds the book you need on paperight.com. For instance, if you're a nurse, you might want them to find you the Newborn Care book.
  3. The outlet enters your name and phone number on paperight.com (it will appear on your print-out), and clicks a button. They are now legally allowed to print out a copy for you.
  4. The outlet downloads and prints out the book, or (if you have a copy already, and a photocopy-licence is available for it on paperight.com) downloads and prints a licence to photocopy it for you. 
  5. You pay the outlet one price that includes their printing costs and the publisher's licence fee (the outlet pays Paperight, and we pay the publisher). This is calculated on the outlet's screen on Paperight.
